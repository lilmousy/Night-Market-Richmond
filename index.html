Richmond Night Market

April 28th - October 9th

The Beginning

Founded 23 years ago by entrepreneur Raymond Cheung, the first Richmond Night Market was held in 2000 at Continental Centre on Cambie Road, and it has grown from those modest beginnings to become a regional mainstay and must-see for visitors and locals.

Featured in The New York Times, the Richmond Night Market is the largest night market in North America and is an international attraction, drawing more than one million people every year.

This Year

Richmond Night Market continues to be a must visit Summer attraction in the Lower Mainland, offering an amazing variety of street food, merchandise and games. Open every weekend from May to October, you certainly don’t want to miss it!

FAQ

Are pets allowed?

Yes, Pets must always remain on a leash, and in full control of the owner. Pet owners are also asked to be kind, respectful and considerate of others attending the Richmond Night Market. Pet owners must also clean up after their pets when they make a mess. Larger dogs must be BOTH leashed AND muzzled, for the safety of visitors, vendors and staff at the Richmond Night Market.

A Feast for the Eyes, and Stomach!

Adorned for the first time with custom-made neon signs, more than 110 food booths and food trucks offer more than 600 menu items. It’s the largest selection of international foods in a single location at Summer Wonderland. New this year, visitors can try churros and butter beer, as well as sampling food from Indonesia and Turkey.
